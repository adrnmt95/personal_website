About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at Ca' Foscari University of Venice. My research sits at the intersection of political economy, causal inference and machine learning.

I work on topics like conflict, voting behavior, and organized crime, using tools from econometrics, network analysis, and machine learning. These days I'm particularly interested in text-as-data methods and how we can use LLMs to better understand political processes.

Before the PhD, I worked at the European Investment Bank and as a research assistant at the University of Turin, where I got into using AI for cultural economics—basically trying to predict museum visitor flows and analyzing how people's cultural habits influence each other.
